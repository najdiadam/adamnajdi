Experience Reflection

Working with Becky's Button on the Rebecca Dykes Changemaker Challenge has been an incredibly meaningful experience that combined my technical skills with social justice advocacy. Developing the automated judging system challenged me to think creatively about how technology can streamline complex processes while maintaining fairness and transparency.

The global reach of this initiative working with a UK-based organization while recruiting participants from Lebanon highlighted the universal nature of the fight against sex-based violence. The challenge of bringing over 12 AUB students to participate required cultural sensitivity and persuasive communication about why this cause matters globally.

The technical problem-solving aspect was particularly rewarding. Creating an integrated system that judges could use efficiently while maintaining data integrity taught me valuable lessons about user experience design and automated systems. This experience reinforced my belief that technology should serve social causes, and that economists can contribute to social justice movements through both analytical thinking and practical technical skills.
