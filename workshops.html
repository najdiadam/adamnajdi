Professional development through international economic and policy forums

August 2025

Youth Economic Security Forum (YES-F)

A premier international forum bringing together youth leaders, policymakers, and academics to address critical issues in economic security, development, and global cooperation. The forum featured high-level sessions with leadership from international organizations, academic institutions, and policy experts.

01

Role of Youth in Economic Development

Islamic Cooperation Youth Forum (ICYF)

An engaging session exploring how youth leadership can drive the creation of resilient economies that ensure financial stability, promote social equity, and advance sustainable development on a global scale. The session emphasized the critical role of young economists and policymakers in shaping inclusive economic frameworks.

Key Focus Areas

  • Youth-driven economic innovation and entrepreneurship
  • Building financial resilience in emerging economies
  • Advancing social equity through inclusive economic policies
  • Sustainable development goals and youth engagement
02

Supply Chains, Sanctions, and Economic Statecraft

Acting President, University of Management and Technology Lahore

A comprehensive examination of how global supply chains, international sanctions, and strategic trade policies function as powerful tools of economic statecraft. The session provided deep insights into strategies nations employ to build economic resilience amid geopolitical tensions and supply chain disruptions.

Key Focus Areas

  • Global supply chain vulnerabilities and restructuring
  • Economic sanctions as foreign policy instruments
  • Trade policy and national security intersections
  • Strategies for economic resilience and self-sufficiency
03

Tourism, Culture, and Soft Economic Security

Dr. Mehmet Ozkan, Maarif Foundation

An insightful exploration of how cultural tourism and cultural diplomacy contribute to economic resilience while simultaneously projecting soft power and fostering sustainable development. The session highlighted the intersection of cultural heritage, economic development, and international relations.

Key Focus Areas

  • Cultural tourism as economic development driver
  • Soft power projection through cultural diplomacy
  • Sustainable tourism models and economic impact
  • Heritage preservation and economic opportunity
04

FinTech and the Future of Economic Sovereignty

Rector, Spain Business School, Madrid

A forward-looking analysis of how financial technology innovations are fundamentally reshaping national economic independence and monetary sovereignty in increasingly digital economies. The session examined the implications of digital currencies, blockchain technology, and decentralized finance for national economic policy.

Key Focus Areas

  • Digital currencies and central bank digital currencies (CBDCs)
  • FinTech disruption and traditional banking systems
  • Monetary sovereignty in the age of cryptocurrencies
  • Regulatory frameworks for financial innovation

Forum Impact & Key Takeaways

The Youth Economic Security Forum provided a unique platform for engaging with thought leaders from international organizations, academic institutions, and government bodies. The sessions offered critical perspectives on the intersection of youth leadership, economic policy, and global security challenges, while fostering meaningful dialogue on innovative solutions for contemporary economic challenges.

October 2025

Global Future Forum (GFF)

An innovative international forum dedicated to exploring the future of global economy, financial technology, and diplomatic relations in the age of artificial intelligence. The forum featured three specialized tracks focusing on economic structures, financial empowerment, and human-centered diplomacy, with outcomes contributing to actionable policy recommendations.

Track 1

Indivisible Economic Security: Structures in the Future Economy

An in-depth examination of how entrepreneurs, corporations, and institutions collaborate to shape resilient and inclusive economies. The track focused on identifying sustainable economic structures that ensure security and opportunity for all stakeholders in an increasingly interconnected global economy.

Track Objectives

  • Analyzing the role of entrepreneurship in building economic resilience
  • Examining corporate governance and institutional frameworks
  • Developing inclusive economic models for diverse populations
  • Contributing insights to forum's draft resolution on economic security
Impact: Discussions surfaced innovative approaches to economic structure design, informing the forum's comprehensive draft resolution on future economic systems.
Track 2

Future of FinTech: Crypto, Digital Assets, and Financial Empowerment

A comprehensive exploration of digital currencies, cryptocurrencies, and financial technology as transformative tools for financial inclusion and economic literacy. The track examined how emerging technologies can democratize financial services and empower underserved populations globally.

Track Objectives

  • Evaluating cryptocurrencies and digital assets in financial systems
  • Exploring FinTech solutions for financial inclusion
  • Advancing financial literacy through technology-enabled platforms
  • Developing practical recommendations for accessible financial services
Impact: Participants generated actionable ideas for leveraging financial technology to expand access to banking services and improve financial literacy worldwide.
Track 3

Diplomacy in the Age of AI: Humanity, Cultures, and Emotional Futures

A thought-provoking analysis of artificial intelligence's implications for culture, identity, and human-centered diplomacy. The track emphasized the importance of ethics, emotional intelligence, and cultural sensitivity in shaping AI governance frameworks and international cooperation.

Track Objectives

  • Examining AI's impact on cultural identity and human values
  • Developing ethical frameworks for AI governance
  • Integrating emotional intelligence into diplomatic strategies
  • Formulating recommendations for human-centered AI development
Impact: The track produced nuanced recommendations balancing technological advancement with cultural preservation and human dignity, guiding ethical AI policy development.

Forum Contributions & Outcomes

The Global Future Forum represented a unique opportunity to contribute to forward-thinking discussions on the intersection of technology, economics, and diplomacy. Through intensive collaborative sessions, participants developed actionable recommendations that informed the forum's comprehensive resolution addressing the future of global cooperation, financial systems, and AI governance. The experience provided invaluable exposure to cutting-edge policy discourse and international collaboration methodologies.
