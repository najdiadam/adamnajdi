Global Future Forum (GFF) 2025

Economic Security, Fintech, and AI Diplomacy
Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

Forum Overview

Selected as a fully funded delegate for the Global Future Forum in Kuala Lumpur, joining 40 participants from over 30 nationalities. Engaged in intensive discussions across three strategic tracks: Economic Security, Future of Fintech, and Diplomacy in the Age of AI.

Key Sessions & Activities

Economic Security Track Presentation

Delivered 18-minute presentation on Lebanon's economy, analyzing four research papers on crime rates, drug trade, renewable energy, and political resource allocation in sectarian economies

Security, Strategy & Reference Points

Participated in foundational lecture by GFF Chair Malik Tahseen on traditional vs non-traditional security, strategic foresight, and navigating volatility and uncertainty

Future of Fintech & Crypto

Explored how financial technology reshapes access to services, with sessions on crypto integration, digital assets, and building resilient, inclusive economies

MUN-Style Strategic Debate

Competed in Model UN debate format, developing resolutions with clauses for Economic Security track. Team placed second through delegate voting after intensive lobbying and presentation

Three Strategic Tracks

"Indivisible Economic Security"

Analyzed power structures in the future economy, examining how traditional and non-traditional security frameworks intersect with economic development and institutional resilience.

"The Future of Fintech"

Examined crypto, digital assets, and financial empowerment, discussing integration strategies for bitcoin and cryptocurrencies in modern economies and their democratizing potential.

"Diplomacy in the Age of AI"

Explored humanity, cultures, and emotional futures in an AI-driven world, examining how artificial intelligence transforms diplomatic relations and human interaction.

Personal Reflection

"The Global Future Forum was an incredible opportunity to present my research on Lebanon's economy and sectarian political systems to an international audience. Discussing my papers on crime rates, drug trade economics, renewable energy adoption, and political resource allocation gave me valuable feedback from delegates representing over 30 countries.

The strategic framework lecture was transformative. Understanding the distinction between traditional and non-traditional security, and learning about the VUCA environment (Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity), gave me new tools for thinking about Lebanon's economic challenges. The concept of 'referent point' particularly resonated - without clarity on what we're trying to achieve, there can be no effective strategy.

Promoting CEF during my presentation generated significant interest from delegates who want to participate or collaborate. The MUN-style debate on economic security sharpened my ability to develop policy resolutions under time pressure while lobbying for support.

The sessions on fintech and crypto challenged my thinking about financial inclusion and digital assets. For Lebanon, where banking sector collapse has frozen deposits, alternative financial systems could offer real solutions. The forum reinforced that youth must be integrated into economic planning - we bring fresh perspectives and digital fluency that traditional institutions lack."
- Adam Najdi

Young Economic Security Forum (YESF) 2025

Centre for International Political Economy (CIPE)
Istanbul, Turkey

Forum Overview

Participated in the prestigious Young Economic Security Forum organized by the Centre for International Political Economy (CIPE) in Istanbul, Turkey. This international forum brought together young leaders, economists, and policymakers from over 50 nationalities to discuss critical global economic challenges and security issues.

Key Sessions & Activities

Expert-Led Sessions

Participated in sessions led by leading experts in economic security, diplomacy, and strategic forecasting

Global Economic Discussions

Engaged in discussions with economists, diplomats and political analysts, analyzing current global processes

Institutional Visits

Visited leading institutions in Istanbul, including the headquarters of ICYF and ASKON

Financial Technologies & Governance

Explored the impact of financial technologies, supply chains, sanctions and economic governance on stability

Standout Moments

"The Role of Youth in Economic Development"

Participated in this session organized with ICYF leadership, discussing how youth leadership can drive sustainable economy combining financial stability, social equality, and sustainable development.

Cultural Tourism & Economic Stability

Explored how cultural tourism and culture can strengthen economic stability, acting as an instrument of soft power and intercultural dialogue.
