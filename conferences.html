Forum Overview

Participated in the prestigious Young Economic Security Forum organized by the Centre for International Political Economy (CIPE) in Istanbul, Turkey. This international forum brought together young leaders, economists, and policymakers from over 50 nationalities to discuss critical global economic challenges and security issues.

Forum Highlights

Personal Reflection

"The Young Economic Security Forum in Istanbul was truly transformative. As a young economist passionate about developmental economics, participating alongside peers from over 50 countries provided invaluable global perspectives on economic security challenges. The session on youth leadership in economic development particularly resonated with my work on Lebanon's economic revitalization through the Global Strategy Review initiative.

What struck me most was the emphasis on cultural tourism as an economic stability tool—a concept directly applicable to Lebanon's diverse cultural heritage. The discussions on financial technologies and supply chains offered new frameworks for thinking about economic resilience, especially relevant given Lebanon's current challenges.

The academic immersion at Istanbul University reinforced my belief that cross-cultural knowledge exchange is essential for generating innovative economic solutions. This forum served as a powerful reminder that dialogue, innovation, and intercultural cooperation are fundamental to building secure and sustainable economic futures. The connections made and insights gained will undoubtedly influence my ongoing research in developmental economics and policy analysis."
- Adam Najdi
