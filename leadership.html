Cedar Economics Forum (CEF) 2025

Lebanon's Premier International Economics Conference

As Conference Chair and Founder of the Cedar Economics Forum, I am leading Lebanon's first large-scale international economics conference that brings together 420-500 participants including students, academics, policymakers, and international experts from across the MENA region and beyond. This groundbreaking initiative establishes AUB as the premier destination for international economic dialogue in the region.

Conference Overview

  • Theme: "The Future of Work in a Digital Economy"
  • Scale: 420-500 participants from 15+ institutions
  • Duration: 4-day international conference (November 27-30, 2025)
  • Location: American University of Beirut
  • Budget: $56,125 total conference budget
  • Reach: 8+ countries across MENA, Europe, and North America

Conference Innovation & Design

The Cedar Economics Forum features an innovative structure that distinguishes it from traditional academic conferences, combining rigorous competition with interdisciplinary collaboration and real-world problem-solving.

Competition Tracks

Track 1: Policy Simulation

Reshaping Education and Labor Policy for a Digital Future

Teams act as advisory committees developing comprehensive policy recommendations for government ministries, including situation analysis, implementation timelines, budget estimates, and success metrics.

Deliverable: 8-page policy brief + 20-minute presentation

Track 2: Business Case Competition

Entrepreneurship and Inclusive Growth in the Digital Era

Teams develop scalable business strategies leveraging technology for economic opportunities, focusing on financial inclusion, youth employment, and sustainable development with complete financial models.

Deliverable: Business plan + investor-style pitch

Track 3: Data & Innovation

Harnessing Data and Technology for Inclusive Economies

Teams analyze real economic datasets from World Bank, ILO, and UNDP to develop technology-enabled solutions with data visualization, algorithm design, and scalability analysis.

Deliverable: Technical report + functional prototype

Interdisciplinary Grand Final

Top two teams from each track (6 teams total) reorganize into 2-3 interdisciplinary synthesis teams combining policy, business, and technology perspectives. Teams integrate their track solutions to address: "How can policy frameworks, business models, and technological solutions work together to accelerate inclusive economic transformation?"

Innovation: Rather than creating new solutions, teams synthesize and integrate existing work to demonstrate cross-sectoral collaboration and implementation pathways.

Long-term Sustainability & Vision

The Cedar Economics Forum is designed as a sustainable annual platform that will grow and evolve over time, establishing a lasting legacy for AUB and the region.

Year 1 (2025)

Foundation

Establish successful model at AUB with strong Lebanese and regional participation. Heavy reliance on sponsorship and institutional support with modest registration fees. Launch Cedar Economics Forum Proceedings.

Years 2-3

Growth & Expansion

Increase registration fees based on demonstrated value. Establish recurring sponsor relationships and pursue government grants. Develop partnerships with other universities for co-hosting or satellite events.

Year 4+

Sustainability & Regional Leadership

Achieve self-sustainability through registrations and sponsorships. Transform proceedings into regionally recognized academic journal. Create rotating hosting model across MENA region while maintaining AUB as permanent secretariat.

Institutional Integration Strategy

  • Faculty Adoption: Formal incorporation into Economics Department annual activities
  • Curriculum Integration: Course projects and research aligned with conference themes
  • Student Organization: Establishment of permanent student society for conference management
  • Administrative Support: Dedicated staff liaison within Office of Student Affairs
  • Research Collaboration: Ongoing faculty and student research emerging from conference themes
